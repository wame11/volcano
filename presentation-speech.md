# 3 Minute Presentation Speech: Volcanic Activity

## Speaker 1: Introduction, about 35 seconds

Hi everyone. Our website is about volcanic activity as a natural cause of climate change. Volcanoes happen when magma rises from inside Earth and escapes through vents or cracks. On the first section, we used a red and orange lava theme because the topic is hot, powerful, and dramatic. The main point is that volcanoes can affect climate, but mostly for a short time.

## Speaker 2: How eruptions work, about 35 seconds

Volcanoes work because pressure builds underground. Magma rises, gases expand, and if the pressure becomes strong enough, the volcano erupts. It can release lava, ash, rocks, steam, carbon dioxide, and sulfur gases. The ash plume image on the site matches this section because it shows how material can be blasted high into the sky during an explosive eruption.

## Speaker 3: Climate effect, about 40 seconds

The most important climate effect comes from sulfur dioxide. After a huge eruption, sulfur dioxide can reach the stratosphere and turn into tiny sulfate aerosols. These particles reflect some sunlight back into space, a bit like a temporary sunshade. This can cool Earth for months or a few years. So volcanoes can cause short-term cooling, not long-term warming.

## Speaker 4: Evidence, about 40 seconds

A strong example is Mount Pinatubo in 1991. Scientists tracked the eruption using satellites and saw the aerosol cloud spread around the world. After that eruption, global average temperature dropped by about 0.5 degrees Celsius for a short time. This evidence supports the idea that powerful volcanoes can affect climate when their gases reach high enough in the atmosphere.

## Speaker 5: Importance and conclusion, about 35 seconds

Volcanoes are important because they show that Earth's climate can change naturally. They can affect air quality, flights, farming, and weather patterns for a short time. But they do not explain today's long-term climate change because their cooling effect fades. Our final takeaway is: volcanoes can change climate fast, but only briefly, while human greenhouse gases cause the longer warming trend.
